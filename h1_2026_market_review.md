# 2026年上半年美股与A股复盘及下半年投资方向

> 数据截止：2026年6月30日（H1收官）。资料来源：新浪财经/中新经纬、新京报、东方财富、东莞证券、StockWire X、Dimensional、Whalesbook、SIACharts、Ashmore、T. Rowe Price、J.P. Morgan、Wellington 等近一两周公开财经报道与机构研报。
>
> 本文仅为信息整理与观点综述，不构成任何投资建议。市场有风险，决策需谨慎。

---

## 一、上半年市场全景

### 1. 美股：AI 驱动的"先抑后扬"，年末出现高位分化

- **指数表现**：标普500（S&P 500）上半年累计上涨约 **7%+**，6月29日收于约 **7,354 点**；道琼斯工业指数收于约 **51,876 点**；纳斯达克综合指数收于约 **25,297 点**。纳指/纳指100上半年涨幅在 **+9% ~ +18%** 区间（口径不同），整体显著强于大盘。
- **走势节奏**：年初受中东地缘冲突、油价上行扰动一度回调（3月明显下跌）→ 5月底至6月初在芯片股带动下创历史新高 → **6月最后一周科技股连续5个交易日下跌、纳指单周大跌约 4.6%，而道指逆势微涨约 0.6%**。
- **核心驱动**：人工智能资本开支。Alphabet、微软、亚马逊、Meta、甲骨文等科技巨头2026全年数据中心与扩张支出预计超 **7,000亿美元**，直接利好英伟达、博通、美光等半导体与存储链。标普500一季度盈利同比增长高达 **28.6%**，为2021年末以来最高。
- **关键信号**：6月末的"纳指跌、道指涨"分化，被市场视为**资金正从高度集中的科技龙头向更广泛板块（周期、金融、工业）轮动**的实时预演——"市场宽度（breadth）"开始扩散。
- **风险点**：通胀有重新抬头迹象，美联储（新任主席领导下）维持利率不变但9月起存在加息可能；10年期美债收益率升至接近 **4.5%**；趋势跟踪基金（CTA）持有约 **930亿美元**净多头，存在机械性抛售（>1000亿美元）的流动性风险。

### 2. A股：极致结构性分化，"科技独领风骚"

- **宽基指数**：上证指数累涨约 **+3.16%**（6月30日收于 4,094.40 点），深证成指 **+19.82%**，创业板指 **+35.58%**，科创50指数 **+64.25%**，上证50反而微跌逾1%。**小盘、成长风格全面占优**。
- **走势节奏（三阶段）**：1–2月春季行情科技领涨、上证一度"17连阳"、单日成交额突破3.6万亿元 → 3–4月初中东地缘冲突引发调整、资金转向高股息防御 → 4月中旬起政策托底叠加盈利修复驱动反弹，三大指数集体收红。
- **行业冰火两重天**：申万31个一级行业仅10个上涨。**电子飙升约86%、通信涨超70%、建筑材料约46%**领涨；**商贸零售、美容护理、农林牧渔、传媒、钢铁、食品饮料、汽车、地产、银行、非银金融等大幅下跌（多数跌超10%–30%）**。
- **个股极端分化**：5,528只个股中上涨逾1,700只、下跌逾3,700只；394只涨幅超100%，但也有大量个股腰斩。寒武纪等算力芯片龙头总市值突破万亿，成为科技狂欢的注脚。
- **交易主线**：全球AI资本开支、国产半导体替代、产业链上游原材料涨价、具备业绩支撑的方向。

## 二、"资金现在都去美国了"——这个判断要分两层看

当前全球资金格局存在**两股看似矛盾、实则并存**的力量：

1. **存量层面：美国仍是超配重地。** 美股占全球股票基准约 **2/3**，全球投资者（含美国本土与国际）对美元资产存在历史性超配。AI叙事+强盈利，使美国在上半年依旧是吸金主力之一。
2. **边际层面：再平衡（rotation）已经启动。** 多家机构（Ashmore、T. Rowe Price、Wellington、J.P. Morgan）指出，**资金正从高度集中的美国资产边际流出，转向新兴市场（EM）**。驱动因素：美元走弱、美国与EM估值差巨大、EM盈利改善、AI/半导体供应链红利。2025年EM股票基金净流入创后疫情新高（EM ETF约880亿美元）；2026年1月iShares新兴市场ETF单月净流入超40亿美元，为2015年以来最强。

> 关键变量是**美元方向**。由于美股体量巨大，"哪怕从美国小幅减配1个百分点，对体量更小的EM都是可观的增量资金"。因此"资金去美国"是存量事实，但"边际增量正在离开美国、流向非美/新兴市场"是更值得关注的趋势。

## 三、下半年（H2 2026）投资方向

### 1. 美股：从"七巨头独大"转向"广度扩散（broadening）"

四大投行（RBC、摩根大通、摩根士丹利、Evercore ISI）方向一致——**继续看涨，但领涨者将更广**。RBC将标普500 12个月目标上调至 **8,150 点**（较6/29约 +10.8%）。

- **保留核心科技/AI敞口**：摩根大通指出"七巨头"相对市场其余部分的市盈率处于**十年最低**，盈利仍在兑现，不宜全面撤离优质科技。
- **选择性轮动至受益于"广度扩散"的板块**：摩根士丹利高信心标的为**可选消费、运输、区域性银行**；多家机构看好**工业、材料、金融、能源**等周期与价值方向。
- **降低拥挤度、强调质量与现金流**：对单一标的/主题设置仓位上限，偏好资产负债表稳健、自由现金流稳定、具备定价权的公司。
- **预留流动性**：警惕"市场管道（plumbing）"风险——准备金管理与国债回购收缩可能在政策利率不变的情况下引发流动性冲击与拥挤交易踩踏。
- **需跟踪的两个变量**：①服务与住房通胀（若再加速→由"按兵不动"转向"加息"）；②流动性操作（准备金/国债回购数据）。

### 2. A股：结构性"慢牛"，紧扣"景气为纲"

- **AI仍是最大主线，但进入"考核盈利能力"阶段**：概念炒作将被舍弃，资金有望切换至**业绩确定、产业趋势可持续**的方向，以及部分有望实现"从0到1"跨越的领域。
- **招商证券主线**：海外算力、国产算力、资源品涨价、商业航天。
- **中信建投主线**：聚焦"算力牛"（AI算力全产业链景气扩散，远未到全面泡沫化）与"复苏牛"（"PPI–外需"驱动）两大景气主线，演绎结构性慢牛。
- **东莞证券**："攻守相济，变中求进"；提示短线大概率进入高波动震荡区间，需消化前期领涨板块的估值兑现压力。

### 3. 资金流向与配置含义

- **全球再平衡利好非美/新兴市场**：若美元维持弱势、美联储不加息，EM（含中国相关资产）财务条件改善、估值修复空间打开；亚洲（尤其受益AI基建的台湾、韩国）被点名为直接受益者。
- **A股有望承接部分边际增量**：作为AI/半导体供应链的重要一环，叠加估值低位与盈利修复，国产算力与资源品方向具备吸引力。
- **核心风控**：美元若因美国增长超预期而走强、地缘波动、AI资本开支降温，均可能逆转EM流入；A股需警惕高位题材股盈利兑现不及预期。

---

## 四、一句话总结

> **上半年：美股靠AI"先抑后扬"，A股"科技独领风骚"，两地共性是AI主线+极致结构分化+资金向龙头集中。下半年：美股从"七巨头"走向"广度扩散"（周期/金融/工业补涨，核心科技不撤），A股走"景气为纲"的结构性慢牛（算力牛+复苏牛）。全球资金存量仍超配美国，但边际正在再平衡、流向非美与新兴市场——美元方向是决定这场迁徙速度的总开关。**

# H1 2026 Review of U.S. & China A-Share Markets and H2 Investment Direction

> Data as of June 30, 2026 (end of H1). Sources: Sina Finance/JWView, Beijing News, East Money, Dongguan Securities, StockWire X, Dimensional, Whalesbook, SIACharts, Ashmore, T. Rowe Price, J.P. Morgan, Wellington, and other public financial reports/research from the past one to two weeks.
>
> This is an informational summary only and does not constitute investment advice. Markets carry risk; invest with caution.

---

## 1. Market Panorama for H1 2026

### 1.1 U.S. Equities: An AI-driven "dip-then-rally," with high-level divergence into year-end

- **Index performance**: The S&P 500 rose about **+7%** in H1, closing near **7,354** on June 29; the Dow closed near **51,876**; the Nasdaq Composite near **25,297**. The Nasdaq / Nasdaq-100 gained roughly **+9% to +18%** for the half (depending on the gauge), clearly outpacing the broad market.
- **Path**: Early-year weakness from Middle East geopolitical conflict and rising oil (notable March drop) → record highs in late May/early June led by chipmakers → **in the final week of June, tech stocks fell for five straight sessions and the Nasdaq dropped about 4.6% for the week, while the Dow edged up about 0.6%**.
- **Core driver**: AI capital expenditure. Tech giants (Alphabet, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, Oracle) are expected to spend over **$700 billion** on data centers and expansion in 2026, directly benefiting semiconductors and memory (Nvidia, Broadcom, Micron). S&P 500 Q1 earnings grew **28.6%** YoY, the highest since late 2021.
- **Key signal**: The late-June "Nasdaq down, Dow up" split is read as a live preview of **capital rotating out of highly concentrated tech leaders into broader sectors** (cyclicals, financials, industrials)—market "breadth" is broadening.
- **Risks**: Inflation showing signs of resurgence; the Fed (under new leadership) held rates but a hike is possible from September; the 10-year Treasury yield rose near **4.5%**; trend-following (CTA) funds held about **$93 billion** net long, posing a mechanical-selling liquidity risk (>$100 billion).

### 1.2 China A-Shares: Extreme structural divergence, with tech in solo command

- **Broad indices**: Shanghai Composite **+3.16%** (closing 4,094.40 on June 30), Shenzhen Component **+19.82%**, ChiNext **+35.58%**, STAR 50 **+64.25%**; the SSE 50 actually fell over 1%. **Small-cap and growth styles dominated across the board.**
- **Path (three stages)**: A Jan–Feb spring rally led by tech (17 consecutive up days for the SSE Composite; single-day turnover topped RMB 3.6 trillion) → a March–early April pullback from Middle East geopolitics with capital rotating into high-dividend defensives → a rebound from mid-April on policy support plus earnings recovery, leaving all three major indices green.
- **Sector "ice and fire"**: Of 31 Shenwan primary industries, only 10 rose. **Electronics surged ~86%, telecom up >70%, building materials ~46%** led; **retail, beauty care, agriculture, media, steel, food & beverage, autos, real estate, banks, and non-bank financials fell sharply (many down 10%–30%)**.
- **Extreme stock dispersion**: Of 5,528 stocks, over 1,700 rose and over 3,700 fell; 394 more than doubled, while many others were halved. AI-chip leader Cambricon topped RMB 1 trillion in market cap—an emblem of the tech frenzy.
- **Trading themes**: Global AI capex, domestic semiconductor substitution, upstream raw-material price hikes, and earnings-backed names.

## 2. "Capital is all going to the U.S. now"—read this on two levels

Two seemingly contradictory yet coexisting forces shape today's global capital map:

1. **Stock level: the U.S. remains heavily overweight.** U.S. equities make up roughly **two-thirds** of global equity benchmarks; both domestic and international investors are historically overweight dollar assets. The AI narrative plus strong earnings kept the U.S. a top magnet for capital in H1.
2. **Marginal level: rotation (rebalancing) has begun.** Multiple firms (Ashmore, T. Rowe Price, Wellington, J.P. Morgan) note **capital flowing at the margin out of highly concentrated U.S. assets toward emerging markets (EM)**. Drivers: a softer dollar, a huge US-vs-EM valuation gap, improving EM earnings, and AI/semiconductor supply-chain tailwinds. 2025 saw post-Covid-record EM equity-fund inflows (~$88 billion into EM ETFs); in January 2026 the iShares EM ETF drew over $4 billion in a single month, its strongest since 2015.

> The key variable is the **dollar's direction**. Because U.S. equities are so large, "even a one-point underweight shift away from the U.S. is a meaningful inflow for the smaller EM asset class." So "capital is in the U.S." is a stock fact, but "marginal flows are leaving the U.S. toward non-U.S./EM" is the trend that matters more.

## 3. H2 2026 Investment Direction

### 3.1 U.S. Equities: From "Magnificent Seven dominance" to "broadening"

Four major firms (RBC, JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley, Evercore ISI) agree on direction—**still bullish, but with broader leadership**. RBC raised its 12-month S&P 500 target to **8,150** (about +10.8% from the June 29 close).

- **Keep core tech/AI exposure**: JPMorgan notes the Magnificent Seven's P/E relative to the rest of the market is at a **ten-year low**, with earnings still delivering—do not abandon quality tech wholesale.
- **Rotate selectively into broadening beneficiaries**: Morgan Stanley's high-conviction picks are **Consumer Discretionary, Transportation, and Regional Banks**; firms broadly favor **Industrials, Materials, Financials, and Energy** (cyclical/value).
- **Reduce crowding; emphasize quality and cash flow**: Cap single-name/theme exposure; prefer strong balance sheets, steady free cash flow, and pricing power.
- **Hold liquidity buffers**: Beware "market plumbing" risk—shrinking reserve management and Treasury buybacks can trigger liquidity shocks and crowded-trade unwinds even with policy rates unchanged.
- **Two variables to track**: (1) services & housing inflation (re-acceleration shifts the Fed from "hold" to "hike"); (2) liquidity operations (reserve/Treasury-buyback data).

### 3.2 China A-Shares: A structural "slow bull," anchored on "prosperity (earnings momentum) first"

- **AI remains the top theme but enters an "earnings-test" phase**: Concept speculation gets discarded; capital should rotate toward **earnings-certain, durable-trend** directions and select "zero-to-one" breakthroughs.
- **China Merchants Securities themes**: overseas compute, domestic compute, resource-price increases, commercial aerospace.
- **CITIC Securities (CSC) themes**: focus on the "compute bull" (AI compute-chain prosperity broadening, far from a full bubble) and the "recovery bull" (driven by "PPI–external demand"), playing out a structural slow bull.
- **Dongguan Securities**: "balance offense and defense, advance through change"; expect high-volatility, range-bound trading near term as gains from earlier leaders face valuation-realization pressure.

### 3.3 Capital Flows and Allocation Implications

- **Global rebalancing favors non-U.S./EM**: If the dollar stays soft and the Fed does not hike, EM (including China-related assets) sees easier financial conditions and valuation-repair room; Asia (especially AI-infrastructure beneficiaries Taiwan and South Korea) is named as a direct beneficiary.
- **A-shares may capture some marginal inflows**: As a key node in the AI/semiconductor supply chain, with low valuations and recovering earnings, domestic-compute and resource themes look attractive.
- **Core risk control**: A stronger dollar (if U.S. growth surprises up), geopolitical volatility, or cooling AI capex could reverse EM inflows; A-shares should watch for high-flying theme stocks missing earnings.

## 4. One-Sentence Summary

> **H1: U.S. equities staged an AI-driven "dip-then-rally," while A-shares saw "tech in solo command." Their shared traits: an AI master theme, extreme structural divergence, and capital concentrating in leaders. H2: U.S. equities shift from "Magnificent Seven" toward "broadening" (cyclicals/financials/industrials catch up, without abandoning core tech), while A-shares run a "prosperity-first" structural slow bull (compute bull + recovery bull). Global capital is still overweight the U.S. at the stock level, but is rebalancing at the margin toward non-U.S. and emerging markets—the dollar's direction is the master switch governing the speed of this migration.**
